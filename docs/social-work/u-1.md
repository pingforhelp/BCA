# UNIT I INTRODUCTION OF SOCIAL WORK

Social: Related to organizations/Society;
Work: The actions we perform;
Social work: The action which we perform for the society called as social work.
Introduction: Social work is a problem-solving profession. It is committed to improve the quality
of human life by using different techniques and strategies. It helps to solve the problems of individuals, groups & communities of different places.

# Definition of social work
Social work is practice based profession concerned with meeting the basic needs of individuals, families, & groups, communities, and society as whole to enhance their individual & collective well-being.

Scope of social work: (SW)

child Developments:
1. This is to ensure healthy growth and development of children.
2. It provides support and education to the children.
3. It also provides resources to parents.

Medical Social Work:
1. Here social work collaborates with professionals to meet the psychological
and emotional needs of patients and their families.
2. Clinical social work provides therapy and counselling to individuals, families
and groups dealing with mental health challenges.

International Social Work:
1. This relates to social issues on a global scale.
They might work in disaster response, international development, advocacy for
human rights, etc.

Social welfare organisations: The process of organising and administering
social work services in both private & public sector is known as social welfare
organisation.

Community organization Social work takes place at community level can be called as Community organization. It includes the problems like water scarcity, poverty, unemployment, education and needs etc. are usually dealt within community organization.
Social Group work. Here the social worker helps the members of a group to develop their capabilities and potentialities. The growth and development of group members is promoted in social group work.

Objectives of social work. To solve psycho-social problems like family conflicts, emotional distress etc.
(Psycho-social problems) To fulfill humanitarian needs like helping the poor, women, old-aged, child etc. fulfill humanitarian needs. To enhance and to improve confidence and to overcome challenges which is infront of us. [Enhance self-esteem].
- To create self-sufficiency.
- To improve positive relationship between families, communities etc. by promoting
understanding.

Strengthens social relations
To ensure social justice by fair distribution of resources and equal
opportunities for all. [Ensure social justice]

Functions of Social Work
* Restoration
* Resource provision

The following are the three important functions of social work:
1. Restoration The social worker help the people to restore their abilities, skills and qualities so that they
also lead a normal life like any others or equitable person in deaf person/child.
2. Resource provision The social workers help the society to be aware of schemes provided
by government to fulfill their needs wants e.g., Providing housing schemes, educational loans etc.
3. Prevention: Here the social workers start counselling youngsters or teenagers for stopping drug addiction, future marriage issues etc.

Social problem When a particular social condition or phenomenon disturbs social order and
hinders (for something to happen) smooth working of social institutions that can
called as social problem. 

Social problems in India: Poverty, Regionalism (Related to a place), Linguism (language related), Casteism, Untouchability, unemployment, Gender inequality, Labour problems, Basic Sanitation, Educational issues.
